04Assumptions & Methodology

Area conversion factors and the reasoning behind every scaled and interpolated figure above.

Area conversion factors

Changing room (per room)60 SFT
Washroom / WC (per WC fixture, women)20 SFT
Urinal (per fixture, men)9 SFT
Locker (per locker, men or women)3 SFT
Staff accommodation (per bed, ground staff)40 SFT
GRE accommodation (per person)60 SFT
Handwash point (per basin)8 SFT
Work station (per desk)30 SFT
Dining area per cover — NBC 2016 Part 416.15 SFT
Shower cabin (per unit, men or women)36 SFT

NBC 2016 Part 4 sets dining area at 1.5 sqm / cover (16.15 SFT/cover). A 65% occupancy factor is applied to key count to arrive at pax. All other factors are standard hospitality BOH planning benchmarks — not confirmed Hilton/Spark figures — and can be edited to override with confirmed figures.

Source & method, tier by tier

60 / 80 / 100 / 150 / 200 keys. Counts and areas are taken directly from the internal Spark standards sheet.
120-key BOH. Linear interpolation between the 100- and 150-key benchmarks.
250 / 300-key BOH. Linear extrapolation, continuing the 150 → 200 key growth rate.
Count-to-area, BOH. Convertible items — lockers, workstations, changing rooms and so on — carry two rows each in BOH Standards: a count and a bold area row (count × SFT-per-unit factor). The Quick Lookup view shows both side by side.
Fixed FOH items. Spark wall (120 SFT), retail / vending machine (140 SFT) and chai cart (80 SFT) stay constant regardless of key count, per design standard.
Dine area, FOH. Pax = 65% × key count; dine area = pax × the NBC dining factor. Scales with key count following NBC 2016.
Scaling FOH items. Reception, social lounge, kitchen, gym and washrooms scale using the BOH total's growth curve, so FOH stays proportionally above BOH at every tier.
Excluded from the BOH total. Staff entry & exit (a binary requirement) and the service lift (a fixture count) are excluded per user instruction.
